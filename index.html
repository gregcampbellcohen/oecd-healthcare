Healthcare Costs as a Percentage of GDP in OECD Countries: 2000 – 2017

About American Healthcare

In the United States, healthcare is provided through private insurers and the government. Because of the extreme burden on overhead introduced by the variability of our coverage options, the United States spends far more than any other highly developed country on healthcare while arriving at health outcomes competitive with developing nations like Bolivia and Barbados. Because our healthcare system is not socialized, we lack the necessary financial incentive to treat health as a public issue. In 2018, Americans lived an average of 78.5 years - more than 3 years less than our neighbors to the north. The United States has recently earned the unusual distinction among highly developed countries of having a life expectancy in decline.

About the OECD

The Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development is an intergovernmental economic organization with 37 member countries. In most respects, with a few notable exemptions (China, New Zealand) and additions (Mexico, Colombia), we consider these countries our peers on the world stage.
